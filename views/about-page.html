Who is Bad Bunny?

The Puerto Rican rapper who is conquering the world with his music and much more...

Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio, professionally known as Bad Bunny, is a highly accomplished Puerto Rican rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and occasional professional wrestler. Renowned for his contributions to the Latin trap and reggaeton genres, Bad Bunny's musical style showcases a unique fusion of diverse elements from various genres. With a distinctive serious intonation and a bold sense of fashion, he emerged onto the music scene in 2016 with his captivating track "Diles," paving the way for a fruitful collaboration with Hear This Music.

Bad Bunny has demonstrated a strong commitment to social issues and humanitarian causes throughout his career. In the aftermath of Hurricane Maria, which devastated Puerto Rico, he openly criticized the inadequate provision of humanitarian aid. In response, he took action by establishing the Good Bunny Foundation, an organization dedicated to providing toys to children in poverty-stricken areas of Puerto Rico. In recognition of his philanthropic efforts, Bad Bunny received a nomination for the "Humanitarian Award of the Year" at Telemundo's inaugural Premios Tu Música Urbano in 2019. This acknowledgment underscored his dedication to making a positive impact on the lives of others. On July 22, 2019, Bad Bunny joined forces with fellow artists, including Residente and Ricky Martin, as well as over half a million Puerto Ricans, in a massive protest against government corruption. Taking to the streets and effectively shutting down the Las Américas Expressway, also known as the Luis A. Ferré Highway, they demanded the resignation of then-Governor Ricardo Rosselló. This display of unity and activism exemplified Bad Bunny's commitment to fostering positive change and holding those in power accountable. While Bad Bunny has been vocal on various social issues, he has not explicitly taken a position regarding the Puerto Rican statehood movement. However, he expressed that he would prefer not to see Puerto Rico become the 51st state. It is important to note that this does not imply advocacy for the Independence movement in Puerto Rico either. Bad Bunny's stance on this matter remains nuanced, reflecting his desire for Puerto Rico's future to be considered thoughtfully and with clarity. In July 2018, Bad Bunny found himself embroiled in controversy when he criticized a nail salon in Asturias, Spain, for refusing to serve him based on his gender. Unfortunately, this sparked homophobic comments and speculation about his own sexuality from some fans. In response, Bad Bunny defiantly addressed the hateful comments, offering a provocative and sarcastic response. However, he later recognized the need to address the situation more responsibly and issued an apology, ultimately deleting his own Twitter account. Furthermore, Bad Bunny has used his platform to confront instances of homophobia and transphobia. In January 2019, he publicly criticized a homophobic tweet made by reggaeton artist Don Omar. During a performance on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon in February 2020, Bad Bunny drew attention to the tragic murder of transgender woman Alexa Negrón Luciano in Puerto Rico. Wearing a shirt that boldly stated "They Killed Alexa. Not a Man in a Skirt," he aimed to challenge the misgendering and discrimination faced by transgender individuals. Ricky Martin has praised Bad Bunny as an icon for the Latin queer community due to his unwavering support for LGBTQ+ individuals, as well as his embrace of drag culture. Bad Bunny's outspokenness and willingness to address social issues have positioned him as a voice for change and inclusivity within the Latin music industry. His advocacy serves as an inspiration to many, as he continues to use his influence to shed light on important matters and promote acceptance and equality.
